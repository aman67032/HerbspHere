Seasonal Wellness Guidance

Ayurvedic recommendations to maintain balance throughout the changing seasons

Spring (Vasant Ritu)

Spring is characterized by increased Kapha dosha. As the earth thaws, there's increased moisture and heaviness in the environment.

Recommended Herbs:

  • Ginger (Adrak) - Helps stimulate digestion and reduce mucus
  • Turmeric (Haldi) - Supports the immune system and reduces inflammation
  • Tulsi (Holy Basil) - Helps clear respiratory passages

Lifestyle Tips:

  • Wake up early and exercise regularly to counter Kapha's sluggish effects
  • Favor warm, light, and slightly spicy foods
  • Reduce dairy, sweets, and heavy foods
  • Practice daily dry brushing (garshana) to stimulate lymphatic flow

Summer (Grishma Ritu)

Summer increases Pitta dosha, bringing heat, intensity, and sharpness to the body and mind.

Recommended Herbs:

  • Mint (Pudina) - Cooling effect on the body
  • Coriander (Dhania) - Reduces internal heat
  • Fennel (Saunf) - Soothes the digestive system

Lifestyle Tips:

  • Stay hydrated with cooling drinks like coconut water and mint tea
  • Favor sweet, bitter, and astringent tastes
  • Reduce spicy, sour, and salty foods
  • Schedule outdoor activities in the cooler parts of the day

Herbal First Aid Guide

Simple herbal remedies for common minor ailments and discomforts

Cough & Cold

Quick Remedy: Ginger, honey, and tulsi tea. Boil fresh ginger and tulsi leaves for 5 minutes, strain, and add honey once cooled slightly.

How it helps: Ginger is warming and expectorant, tulsi has antiviral properties, and honey soothes throat irritation.

Headache

Quick Remedy: Peppermint oil temple massage or coriander seed tea. For the tea, steep 1 teaspoon of crushed coriander seeds in hot water for 10 minutes.

How it helps: Peppermint provides cooling relief while coriander seeds help regulate circulation to the head.

Digestive Discomfort

Quick Remedy: Fennel and cumin seed tea. Mix equal parts of both seeds, lightly crush, and steep in hot water for 10 minutes.

How it helps: This combination reduces bloating, gas, and improves overall digestive function naturally.

Skin Irritation

Quick Remedy: Aloe vera gel mixed with a few drops of lavender essential oil, applied topically.

How it helps: Aloe vera cools and soothes irritated skin, while lavender has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our expert consultation services

How do I prepare for a consultation?

To make the most of your consultation, please have your medical history, current medications, and any recent test results ready. Also write down your specific concerns and questions in advance. If possible, take photos of any plants or herbal preparations you want to discuss.

What happens during a video consultation?

During the 60-minute video session, the expert will first discuss your health history and concerns. They will then perform an assessment based on Ayurvedic principles, recommend appropriate herbal remedies, and provide guidance on lifestyle modifications. You'll receive a follow-up email with a summary of recommendations.

How many photos should I submit for plant identification?

For accurate identification, please submit 3-5 clear photos of each plant showing different parts: whole plant, leaves, flowers/fruits, and stem. Include close-ups of distinctive features and indicate the plant's size, habitat, and any notable characteristics like smell.

Are these consultations a replacement for medical care?

No, our expert consultations are complementary to, not a replacement for, conventional medical care. Our Ayurvedic practitioners provide guidance on traditional herbal remedies and practices, but you should continue working with your primary healthcare provider, especially for serious conditions.

What is your cancellation policy?

You can reschedule or cancel your appointment up to 24 hours before the scheduled time without any charge. For cancellations less than 24 hours in advance, a 50% fee will apply. No-shows will be charged the full consultation fee.

How soon will I receive my plant identification or remedy review?

Plant identifications and remedy reviews are typically completed within 48-72 hours of submission. For urgent requests, please indicate this in your submission and we will do our best to expedite the process.
